# Positioning Competitiveness Research

Status: Research draft
Last updated: 2026-06-02

## Purpose

This document evaluates whether Lingual's proposed US and Korea positioning is
competitive against the current public competitor landscape.

The two positioning statements tested:

1. US:
   > Teachers assign curriculum-linked conversations, students practice, and
   > Lingual turns the speaking into debriefs, class evidence, and next-step
   > teaching decisions.

2. Korea:
   > A conversation-native speaking evidence layer for process-based
   > assessment, custom curriculum, and teacher-ready class reporting.

This is not a final GTM plan. It is a research-backed competitiveness read.

## Do We Know Competitors Well Enough?

Short answer: enough for first positioning and pilot strategy, not enough for
pricing, procurement, or final product roadmap confidence.

What we know well enough:

- The core direct/near competitors in the US school market: Speakable,
  Speakology, Telo, Extempore, Nualang, ELSA Schools, plus adjacent products
  such as LinguaPal, Gakimas, OracyAI, FLOW Speak, Quippy, and Kuliso.
- The core direct/near competitors in Korea: Plang School, EBS AI PengTalk, LG
  CNS Speaking Class / AI Digital Class, YBM / Visang AIDT, and private English
  speaking references such as Readyspeak and Yoon's / Samsung English style
  products.
- The baseline product expectations: teacher assignment, student practice,
  instant feedback, transcripts/recordings, dashboards, rubrics, reports,
  privacy language, and some roster/LMS path.

What we do not yet know well enough:

- Actual paid pricing and discounting behavior.
- District procurement conversion paths.
- Private demo-only workflows hidden behind sales calls.
- Real classroom quality: latency, speech recognition accuracy, teacher trust,
  student comfort, and implementation friction.
- Whether competitor "next-step" analytics are real instructional
  recommendations or mostly dashboard summaries.
- Korea public-school procurement path after the AI Digital Textbook policy
  shift.

## US Market Positioning Read

### Position Tested

> Teachers assign curriculum-linked conversations, students practice, and
> Lingual turns the speaking into debriefs, class evidence, and next-step
> teaching decisions.

### Competitiveness Score

**6.5 / 10 as a message. 8 / 10 if the product can prove the full loop in a
pilot.**

The message is directionally strong, but not automatically unique. The US
market already has competitors that can claim parts of it.

### Why This Position Is Viable

US world-language and ESL buyers already understand the problem: students need
more speaking practice, teachers cannot listen to everyone enough, and schools
need evidence tied to standards, rubrics, or proficiency goals.

This market is validated by competitors:

- Speakable shows mature assignment, rubric, grading, LMS, and portfolio
  workflow.
- Speakology shows visible AI conversation, oral exam, transcript, score, and
  gradebook workflow.
- Telo shows a sharp ELL/ML speaking-time wedge with teacher insight claims.
- Extempore shows the assessment/reporting infrastructure bar.
- Nualang shows teacher-authored roleplays, chatbots, transcripts, reports, and
  school distribution through Wayside.
- ELSA shows that pronunciation and speech analytics are already table stakes.
- Newer or adjacent entrants such as Gakimas, OracyAI, DAHUA AI, and LinguaPal
  show that "every student speaks" and "teacher dashboard" are becoming
  crowded claims.

ACTFL also publicly offers an AI conversation classroom activity, which means
AI conversation itself is becoming normalized rather than exotic.

### Where The Position Is Weak

The phrase "curriculum-linked conversations" is not enough by itself. Several
competitors already say they support custom topics, ACTFL/WIDA/CEFR alignment,
rubrics, gradebooks, and teacher dashboards.

The most dangerous direct overlaps:

| Competitor | Why it overlaps |
| --- | --- |
| Speakology | AI conversation, custom topics, ACTFL level, transcripts, feedback, gradebook. |
| Speakable | Teacher assignment workflow, rubrics, grading, LMS/grade sync, simulated conversations. |
| Telo | ELL speaking time, curriculum/textbook compatibility, standards-aligned dashboard claims. |
| Nualang | Teacher-created roleplays/chatbots, reports, transcripts, LMS/SIS/SSO surface. |
| Gakimas | Very close "every student speaks" and "see it on your curriculum" message. |
| Extempore | School assessment, rubrics, common assessments, reports, and LMS infrastructure. |

### Where Lingual Can Still Win

The strongest US wedge is not "AI conversation" or "auto feedback." It is:

> Conversation-to-instruction loop.

The competitive version of the position should emphasize the output after
practice, not just the practice itself.

Recommended US wording:

> Lingual lets teachers assign curriculum-linked AI conversations and turns
> every student session into debriefs, transcript evidence, class patterns, and
> next-step teaching recommendations.

Why this is stronger:

- "Assign" speaks to teacher workflow.
- "Curriculum-linked" avoids generic AI chat.
- "Every student session" speaks to scale.
- "Transcript evidence" speaks to accountability.
- "Class patterns" speaks to teacher usefulness.
- "Next-step teaching recommendations" is the whitespace competitors often
  imply but do not clearly prove.

### US Segment Read

| Segment | Position fit | Competitive risk | Read |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Independent high school world language | High | Medium | Best first US segment. Fastest pilot path and strong teacher pain. |
| Public high school world language | Medium-high | High | Good need, but procurement/privacy/LMS expectations rise quickly. |
| ESL/ELD / multilingual learners | Medium | High | Need is large, but Telo, ELSA, Kuliso, OracyAI, and WIDA-aligned claims create pressure. |
| College language programs | Medium | High | Speakology, Flowent, Talio, LinguaPal, and LMS expectations are strong. Later phase. |
| Language institutes | Medium-high | Medium | Faster revenue path, but school-evidence positioning may need packaging changes. |

### US Must-Prove Product Claims

To make the position competitive, the pilot demo must prove:

1. A teacher can create or choose a conversation tied to a unit/objective.
2. Students can complete natural enough speaking practice without heavy setup.
3. Students receive an understandable debrief.
4. Teachers see transcript evidence and class-level patterns.
5. The teacher can decide what to reteach or assign next.
6. A pilot report can summarize speaking time, completion, session length,
   sample debriefs, teacher/student feedback, and implementation friction.

Without these proof points, Lingual will look like another AI conversation tool.

## Korea Market Positioning Read

### Position Tested

> A conversation-native speaking evidence layer for process-based assessment,
> custom curriculum, and teacher-ready class reporting.

### Competitiveness Score

**5.5 / 10 for direct Korea public-school sales today. 7 / 10 for pilots or
partner-led entry. 8 / 10 if paired with strong Korean assessment language and
local procurement path.**

The product gap is real, but the route to adoption is harder than in the US.

### Why This Position Is Viable

Korea is not empty, but that also means the need is validated.

Important market signals:

- EBS AI PengTalk establishes that public schools already know AI English
  speaking practice.
- Plang School proves that Korean schools value AI feedback, teacher workflow,
  process-centered assessment, and student activity evidence.
- LG CNS proves that education offices can adopt AI English speaking services
  at scale and that teacher-created AI speaking content is a known concept.
- YBM / Visang AIDT prove that textbook-linked AI workflows, dashboards,
  personalized learning, and teacher reports are already part of school
  expectations.
- Korea's AI Digital Textbook status shifted toward optional educational
  materials, which weakens "official textbook replacement" positioning but may
  create room for supplementary tools if procurement is handled correctly.

### Where The Position Is Weak

The English phrase "conversation-native speaking evidence layer" is strategically
correct but probably not the best Korea-facing language.

Korean public-school buyers are more likely to care about:

- 과정중심평가 support.
- 수행평가 evidence.
- 학생별 피드백.
- 교사 업무 경감.
- 세특 or activity-record support where appropriate.
- 수준별 맞춤 지도.
- 교육과정/교과서 연계.
- 개인정보/학교 도입 friction.

The biggest Korea risk is not product-market need. It is channel and trust.
EBS, Plang, LG CNS, YBM, and Visang all have stronger local legitimacy.

### Where Lingual Can Still Win

Lingual should not position as:

- A replacement for EBS AI PengTalk.
- Another AI Digital Textbook.
- A generic AI English speaking app.
- A pronunciation scoring product.

The sharper Korea position is:

> Teacher-designed AI speaking activities that produce process-assessment
> evidence, student debriefs, and class reports from real conversations.

Korea-facing version:

> 교사가 수업 목표에 맞춰 AI 말하기 활동을 설계하고, 학생의 대화 결과를
> 피드백, 수행평가 근거, 수업 리포트로 전환하는 말하기 수업 도구.

This is more competitive than "AI conversation" because it frames Lingual as a
teacher workflow and assessment evidence tool.

### Korea Segment Read

| Segment | Position fit | Competitive risk | Read |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Korea public elementary/middle | Medium | Very high | Long-term upside, but district/government path and local trust are major blockers. |
| Korea education-office pilot | Medium-high | High | Possible if framed as supplementary speaking evidence, not AIDT replacement. |
| Korea private language institutes | Medium-high | Medium | Faster entry; custom curriculum and reporting may matter more than procurement. |
| Korea international / bilingual programs | Medium-high | Medium | Better early fit if English-first reporting and school workflow matter. |
| Korea public high school English | Medium | High | Speaking performance/evidence need exists, but exam-centered pressure and procurement matter. |

### Korea Must-Prove Product Claims

To make the position competitive in Korea, Lingual must prove:

1. Teacher activity creation from a lesson objective or textbook/curriculum
   unit.
2. Natural conversation, not only reading aloud or sentence drills.
3. Student debriefs in language teachers trust.
4. Teacher-visible evidence that can support formative assessment or
   performance assessment.
5. Class-level reporting that reduces teacher work.
6. Clear privacy, data retention, and school-admin controls.
7. A local sales/procurement route: school pilot, institute pilot, education
   office partner, or publisher/platform partner.

## Jeopardy / Class Activity Implication

The class-level Jeopardy-style flow may be more strategically useful than it
first appears.

Proposed flow:

1. Students complete curriculum-linked AI conversations.
2. Each student generates or selects questions from their conversation.
3. The class plays a team-based quiz/Jeopardy round using those questions.
4. Students solve, explain, negotiate meaning, and reuse target language.
5. Lingual captures speaking/listening evidence and produces teacher-facing
   debriefs.

Why it matters:

- It converts isolated AI practice into a classroom activity.
- It creates visible peer learning, not just private chatbot time.
- It gives the teacher a performance-assessment artifact.
- It differentiates from products that stop at individual feedback.
- It makes the pilot demo memorable.

US framing:

> From individual AI practice to collaborative class evidence.

Korea framing:

> 개별 AI 말하기 연습을 수업 활동과 수행평가 근거로 연결.

## Final Positioning Recommendation

### US

Use:

> Curriculum-linked AI speaking assignments that turn every student conversation
> into debriefs, transcript evidence, class patterns, and next-step teaching
> recommendations.

Avoid:

> AI conversation practice for language learners.

Reason:

The generic AI conversation market is crowded. The defensible layer is teacher
workflow plus evidence plus instructional decision support.

### Korea

Use:

> Teacher-designed AI speaking activities that turn student conversations into
> process-assessment evidence, student feedback, and class reports.

Avoid:

> AI English speaking app.

Reason:

Korea already has free public AI speaking, strong school assessment competitors,
and textbook-linked AI platforms. Lingual must look like a speaking evidence
tool for teachers, not another app.

## Next Research Needed

1. **Competitor demo access**
   Get trial/demo access or sales demos for Speakable, Speakology, Plang,
   Nualang, ELSA Schools, Telo, and Visang/YBM where possible.

2. **Pilot buyer interviews**
   Interview 5-10 US world-language teachers and 5-10 Korean English teachers
   or institute operators around the exact wording of the positioning.

3. **Procurement route research**
   Separate legal/procurement work for US districts and Korean public-school
   education-office adoption.

4. **Pricing research**
   Research teacher-seat, student-seat, school-license, and pilot-to-paid
   conversion structures.

5. **Jeopardy/class activity validation**
   Test whether teachers see the collaborative question-sharing format as
   valuable or as extra classroom overhead.
