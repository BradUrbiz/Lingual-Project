# Plang School Market Position Research

Status: Focused competitor research draft
Last updated: 2026-06-02

## Purpose

This memo answers one narrow Korea-market question:

> How strong is Plang School as a competitor, how far has it progressed in the Korean school market, and how much of a barrier does the Seoul Metropolitan Office of Education signal create for Lingual?

This is a business-facing research note. It is not technical analysis, legal advice, or a final procurement conclusion.

## Executive Read

Plang School should be treated as Lingual's highest-priority Korea competitor for public-school English adoption.

The key reason is not that Plang has an AI speaking app. The stronger point is that Plang School has moved from consumer-style AI English learning into Korean school workflows: process-centered assessment, performance assessment, teacher feedback, student activity evidence, and special-activity-record language.

Current read:

- **Market position:** Tier 1 Korean school competitor.
- **Category ownership:** English process-centered assessment plus AI-supported classwork.
- **Adoption progress:** Strong public-school signals in Seoul and Jeonnam, plus Gyeonggi Hi-Learning data-sharing evidence.
- **Procurement barrier:** High, but not yet fully quantified. The Seoul signal is real enough to matter, but the exact contract route, contract value, usage scope, and renewal mechanics still need procurement-record validation.
- **Lingual implication:** Do not compete as a generic AI English speaking app. Compete as a conversation-native speaking evidence layer that produces teacher-usable class reports from real multi-turn practice.

## What Plang School Appears To Own

### 1. Korean School Vocabulary

Plang School's strongest business asset is not only product functionality. It speaks the vocabulary Korean English teachers and school buyers already use:

- `과정 중심 평가`
- `수행평가`
- `서술형/논술형 평가`
- `AI + 교사 피드백`
- `세부능력 특기사항`
- student activity evidence
- teacher workload reduction

Official messaging leads with "영어 과정 중심 평가를 편하게" and "AI와 함께하는 수업과 평가." The site presents writing, reading, speaking, and listening as one school workflow rather than separate consumer practice tools.

### 2. Four-Skill Assessment Platform, Not Just Speaking

Plang School covers:

- **Writing:** AI feedback, teacher feedback, handwriting scoring, grammar/error checking, and criteria-based scoring.
- **Reading:** teacher-uploaded passages, AI-generated questions, real-time student responses, and class participation visibility.
- **Speaking:** pronunciation and content feedback, repeated recording, speaking assessment, and practice loops.
- **Listening:** script/question generation and process-oriented listening assessment.

This matters because Korean public-school buyers may not buy "conversation practice" as a standalone category. They may buy a tool that makes English class assessment and documentation easier.

### 3. Education-Office Adoption Signals

The strongest public signals found:

- A March 26, 2026 report says Plang signed a second-year supply contract with Seoul Metropolitan Office of Education after first-year adoption, citing positive teacher response and fit for speaking/writing assessment.
- Plang hosts a dedicated `플랭스쿨 X 서울시교육청` page with a link to Seoul AIEP.
- A May 11, 2026 report says Jeollanam-do Office of Education signed a supply contract, connected Plang School to `아이모두`, and planned free distribution to all schools in Jeonnam during 2026.
- A Gyeonggi elementary school privacy policy lists `㈜플랭` as one of the providers receiving student data for Hi-Learning service use.

Interpretation: Plang is not only running teacher-by-teacher pilots. It appears to be building the exact kind of education-office and platform-linked path Lingual would eventually need in Korea.

## Why The Seoul Contract Matters

The Seoul signal is strategically important for three reasons.

### 1. Seoul Gives Legitimacy

If a Korean public-school buyer asks "has anyone like us already used this?", Plang can point to Seoul. That makes Plang feel safer than a new entrant, especially for a product handling student data, AI feedback, and school assessment workflows.

### 2. AIEP Integration May Reduce Friction

The dedicated Plang Seoul page links into Seoul's AIEP login flow. Even if the integration depth is not fully visible publicly, this suggests Plang has some distribution path through an education-office platform. That is much harder to beat than a standalone SaaS login.

### 3. Renewal Signal Is Stronger Than A One-Time Pilot

The media report describes Seoul as a second-year supply relationship. If accurate, this is more important than a one-semester trial because it implies enough first-year satisfaction to continue.

The caution: this pass did not find the public procurement record, contract value, usage terms, or award method. So the Seoul relationship is a real competitive signal, but not yet a fully mapped procurement precedent.

## Where Plang May Be Vulnerable

The main gap is not obvious from their marketing, so this needs hands-on validation.

Likely vulnerabilities:

- **Conversation depth may be limited.** Public speaking examples emphasize reading aloud, repeated recording, pronunciation/fluency scoring, one-minute speaking, and roleplay after practice. Public evidence does not yet prove rich open-ended multi-turn AI conversation.
- **It may be broad rather than deep.** Plang covers all four English skills. That is powerful for school assessment, but it may leave room for Lingual to be the deeper conversation-practice specialist.
- **Language scope appears English-first.** Public school positioning is centered on English. If Lingual's long-term school story includes multiple target languages, Plang may not block every segment.
- **Procurement/compliance details are not transparent.** Public evidence did not show school pricing, contract route, voice/audio policy depth, CSAP, ISMS-P, data-localization posture, or admin-grade controls.
- **Teacher next-step intelligence is unclear.** Plang shows feedback and evidence. It is less clear whether it turns class data into "what should I reteach tomorrow?" instructional recommendations.

## Lingual Response

Lingual should not try to become Plang with fewer features. That would be the wrong fight.

Recommended Korea-facing position:

> 교사가 수업 목표에 맞춰 AI 말하기 활동을 설계하고, 학생의 실제 대화 결과를 피드백, 수행평가 근거, 수업 리포트로 전환하는 말하기 수업 도구.

Business translation:

> Teacher-designed AI speaking activities that produce process-assessment evidence, student debriefs, and class reports from real conversations.

What to emphasize against Plang:

- Real multi-turn speaking practice, not only recording, repetition, or pronunciation scoring.
- Teacher-set objectives, target expressions, rubrics, and scenario constraints.
- Student debriefs from actual conversation.
- Class-level patterns that help teachers decide what to reteach.
- Pilot reports designed for principals, department leads, and education-office conversations.
- Clear Korean-language privacy, retention, deletion, and consent materials.

What not to claim yet:

- Better than Plang for Korean process assessment.
- Seoul/education-office procurement readiness.
- Full Korean public-sector compliance.
- Integration with AIEP, Digital Onepass, Hi-Learning, `아이모두`, or NEIS.
- Certified assessment scoring.

## Strategic Recommendation

For Korea, Lingual's first public-school-facing wedge should be narrower than Plang's:

1. Start with speaking-heavy English pilots where the teacher wants real conversation practice, not a full four-skill assessment suite.
2. Package the output as process-assessment evidence and class reporting.
3. Produce a Korean pilot report template before serious outreach.
4. Treat education-office adoption as the long-term route, but use school or institute pilots to generate proof first.
5. Build a partner hypothesis: Korean publisher, education-office platform, local edtech reseller, or research/university channel.

The near-term objective is not to "beat Plang in Korea" broadly. It is to prove a wedge Plang may not fully own:

> richer conversation practice that becomes teacher-usable evidence.

## Open Questions For Next Pass

- Can Plang teachers create open-ended multi-turn conversation assignments, or are speaking tasks mostly structured recording and assessment?
- What was the exact Seoul procurement route: open tender, direct award, AIEP vendor selection, pilot renewal, or service supply agreement?
- What was the exact Jeonnam procurement route and contract scope?
- How many Seoul schools/classes/students actually used Plang in year one?
- Is Plang integrated into AIEP only for login/distribution, or also roster, assignment, reporting, and data exchange?
- How does Plang handle raw audio, transcripts, student identifiers, retention, and deletion?
- Does Plang hold CSAP, ISMS-P, or another public-sector security posture?
- What are school pricing, seat limits, voice limits, and renewal terms?
- Does Plang support languages beyond English in the school product?
- Can Lingual obtain a teacher demo, webinar recording, or trial access for direct product teardown?
